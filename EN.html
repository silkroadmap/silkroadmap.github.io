Introduction of the Ancient Silk Road

The Silk Road was a network for political, economic, and cultural exchanges between ancient China and other civilizations through overland and maritime routes. This route network is 10,000 kilometers from east to west and 3,000 kilometers from north to south. The Silk Road has a long history and contains wealthy historical value and cultural significance.

● During the Western Han Dynasty, Zhang Qian made two missions to the Western Regions and reached Central Asia and West Asia. Chinese and Western civilizations have established a stable relationship. The Continental Silk Road on land was thus opened. It started from Chang'an (present-day Xi'an) and Luoyang, and traveled through Gansu and Xinjiang to Central Asia, Western Asia, and Europe.

● Until the mid-Tang, the Continental Silk Road was the most important network for exchanges between China and foreign countries. Materials, culture, art, and religions from all over the world converged in the capital city of Chang'an. Therefore, the Tang Dynasty was one of the most tolerant and open period in the Chinese history.

● In the second half of Tang Dynasty, the political environment in northern China was changed by the An Lushan Rebellion. The trades and population shifted the southeast coast of China. The Maritime Silk Road gradually replaced the Continental Silk Road.

● During the Northern Song Dynasty, the Custom Department, namely Shibosi, was set up in Guangzhou, Quanzhou and other places to manage maritime trade. Silk, porcelain and other goods produced in China were exported overseas. The development of foreign trade also played a key role in the prosperity of the Maritime Silk Road.

● In the early Ming Dynasty, Zheng He conducted seven voyages, which were the most important maritime activities in ancient Chinese history. His voyages shaped the international political order and maritime commercial situation at the time.

● During the Ming and Qing Dynasties, the maritime trade covered Asia, Europe, Africa, and the Americas, truly forming a global trade network. The world has thus become a community of shared development.
